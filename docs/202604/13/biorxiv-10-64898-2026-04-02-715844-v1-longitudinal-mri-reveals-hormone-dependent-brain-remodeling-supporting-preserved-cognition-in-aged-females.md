## 摘要
适应性可塑性是大脑随年龄增长抵御结构性衰退并维持功能的能力，是成功老龄化的标志，但其生物学基础仍不清楚。在本研究中，我们结合了跨越雌性和雄性大鼠整个生命周期的纵向磁共振成像（MRI）、电生理学、免疫组化和行为测试，以确定这一过程背后的关键区域和系统水平机制。静息态功能 MRI 揭示了前部脑区一种显著的性别特异性连接重组模式，该模式在中年时期出现，且在雌性中更为显著。微结构 MRI 和组织学分析将连接性的增加与雌性前额叶皮层中更持久的白质保留以及下游神经元功能的维持联系起来，而电生理记录则显示老年雌性同一区域的有效连接有所增强。在行为上，增强的前部连接与更优越的记忆表现相关。在白质成熟的关键时间点进行卵巢切除术损害了这种雌性特异性的神经保护作用，破坏了微结构完整性和功能重组，从而凸显了性激素在塑造这些轨迹中的作用。总之，这些发现确定了前部脑区一种新型的性别二态性功能重组模式，并指出关键时期的雌激素可用性是大脑衰老的一个关键调节因子。

## Abstract
Adaptive plasticity, the brains capacity to counteract structural decline and preserve performance with age, is a hallmark of successful aging, yet its biological underpinnings remain poorly understood. Here, we combined longitudinal magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) spanning the entire lifespan with electrophysiology, immunohistochemistry, and behavioral assays in female and male rats to identify key region- and systems-level mechanisms underlying this process. Resting-state functional MRI revealed a striking sex-specific pattern of connectivity reorganization in anterior brain regions, emerging in midlife and more pronounced in females. Microstructural MRI and histological analyses linked increased connectivity to prolonged white matter preservation and downstream maintenance of neuronal function in the female prefrontal cortex, while electrophysiological recordings demonstrated enhanced effective connectivity in the same region in aged females. Behaviorally, enhanced anterior connectivity was associated with superior memory performance. Ovariectomy at a critical time point for white matter maturation compromised this female-specific neuroprotection, disrupting microstructural integrity and functional reorganization, thereby highlighting the role of sex hormones in shaping these trajectories. Together, these findings identify a novel sexually dimorphic pattern of functional reorganization in anterior brain regions and point to estrogen availability during critical periods as a key modulator of brain aging.
